Croissant

Croissants may be the flaky French darlings of breakfast, but their buttery drama actually begins in Austria, where they started life as humble kipferls—possibly introduced to France by Marie Antoinette herself (because of course she’d bring snacks). Shaped like a crescent to celebrate victory over the Ottomans, packed with at least 25% butter (no margarine allowed, merci), and puffed up by steamy science magic, these golden pastries are as much art as breakfast. Whether you're team almond, chocolate-stuffed, or cronut-curious, just remember: in France, the straight ones are the buttery royalty, and if you’re really living your best life, you’re tearing yours in half and dunking it in coffee like a très chic rebel.
